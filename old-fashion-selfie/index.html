Old fashion selfie

Concept

For Peter Weibel the boundaries of the world are the boundaries of our interfaces do. We do not interact with the world, only with the interface to the world and Maps are interfaces between humans and the World. As inhabitant in it, we only can observe it from inside.
Old fashion selfie is a photography map, it gives pixels pieces of information to navigate images counteracting the fast consumption of an image.

Tech

It’s a device thought to be obsolete and still be functional, thanks to E-ink when the battery would be over, if no recharge, the image will still be present. The pictures shown in old fashion selfie are encrypted in layered, split tiles. Filtered images present information with different levels of pixelation, which are related to the zoom of the image. Only on the last level of the image, we can appreciate the original pixels of the images and all the tiny details kept on them.

Context

As maps work, the first impression just gives us hint of what is contained in the following images, the more detailed information we want to receive, the “closer” we have to augment, therefore reducing the proportional scale of the represented territory. Snapshots are treated as maps, processing information not by only augmenting the scale of the images, but creating abstractions of the subject using the bricks that build digital images. They depict every gesture as a mountain and every line as river, making the imagination to work as when looking at a very old picture, trying to guess what is the story behind every detail, making a journey through the image as a walking trip, slowly travelling through territory accidentals. The represented subject intimacy is protected with this kind of encryption. Only the human mind can put together and reproduce the whole picture mentally. Everything seen shows special but at the same time, very similar to the standards our vocabulary can describe. When a face is represented, we can appreciate the similarities with other equals, two eyes, one nose, mouth and chin. If for Viléms Flusser´s photography was capturing the passing river aka time, todays selfies symbolizes the pressured water torrent each person leaks into the social networks frameworks. The consumerism of media, polemic trends like Facebook which will at some point or another will involve us point to make photography and video obsolete in favor of more immersive media such as the combination of both previous mediums together with physical gestures embodiment. How do we look at pictures nowadays and how generations prior to us used to look at them has significantly changed. Its impossible to measure how much attention people were putting into pictures when photography was invented. If we would compare it with today’s scrolling media consumption, we can figure out what is the difference we are talking about. What is the problem of looking at pictures so fast? Everything must be catchy, then pixel perfection is required(smiles, faces, bodies, landscapes) and we have to produce massive of this content to feed the social machinery that keep us feeling connected to other peoples through mainly, screens. Artificial scarcity works as the artistic principle, creating a gamification of the glance over the different levels, realizing about the simplicity of the logic that keep our brain interested and realizing how we look at images in our everyday life. 
Mario Romera 2022
